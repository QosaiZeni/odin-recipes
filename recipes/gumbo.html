The Halal Gumbo Recipe

Halal gumbo is a delightful and flavorful dish that respects Islamic dietary laws while preserving the rich, hearty essence of traditional gumbo. This version of the classic Louisiana stew incorporates halal meats, such as chicken and beef sausage, instead of the commonly used pork products. The base of the gumbo is a dark, rich roux made from flour and oil, which is cooked until it reaches a deep brown color. The dish is further enriched with a medley of vegetables including onions, bell peppers, and celery, often referred to as the "holy trinity" in Creole cuisine. For added depth, spices like cayenne pepper, thyme, and bay leaves are added, alongside a generous amount of garlic. The halal gumbo is then simmered slowly, allowing all the flavors to meld together beautifully.

To prepare a delicious halal gumbo, start by making the roux with equal parts flour and vegetable oil, stirring constantly until it achieves a chocolate brown hue. Add the chopped onions, bell peppers, and celery, and cook until they soften. Incorporate minced garlic, followed by diced halal chicken and sliced beef sausage, cooking until browned. Sprinkle in your spices, then add chicken broth, tomatoes, and okra, which will thicken the stew naturally. Allow the gumbo to simmer for at least an hour, stirring occasionally, until it reaches the desired consistency. Serve it hot over a bed of fluffy white rice, garnished with fresh parsley. This halal gumbo not only respects dietary restrictions but also delivers an authentic, comforting taste that is sure to satisfy.

Ingredients

    Base Ingredients:

  • White Rice
  • Vegetable Oil: 1/2 cup
  • All-Purpose Flour: 1/2 cup

    • Vegitables:

  • Onion: 1 large, diced
  • Green Bell Pepper: 1, diced
  • Celery: 2 stalks, diced
  • Garlic: 4 cloves, minced
  • Okra: 2 cups, sliced (optional but traditional)

    • Proteins:

  • Chicken: 1 pound, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • Beef Sausage: 1 pound, sliced

    • Liqueds:

  • Chicken Broth: 6 cups
  • Diced Tomatoes: 1 can (14.5 ounces) with juices

    • Spices and Seasonings:

  • Thyme: 1 teaspoon
  • Bay Leaves: 2
  • Cayenne Pepper: 1/2 teaspoon
  • Salt and Pepper: To taste

    • Serving:

Steps

    Prepare and Cook the Roux:

  1. In a large pot, heat 1/2 cup vegetable oil over medium heat.
  2. Gradually whisk in 1/2 cup flour.
  3. Cook, stirring constantly, until the roux turns a dark brown color (about 20-30 minutes).

    4. Add Vegetables and Meats:

  4. Add 1 large diced onion, 1 diced green bell pepper, 2 diced celery stalks, and 4 minced garlic cloves to the roux.
  5. Cook until the vegetables are softened (about 5-7 minutes).
  6. Add 1 pound of cut halal chicken and 1 pound of sliced halal beef sausage, cooking until browned (about 5-7 minutes).

    7. Season the Gumbo:

  7. Stir in 1 teaspoon thyme, 2 bay leaves, 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper, and salt and pepper to taste.

    8. Add Liquids and Vegetables:

  8. Pour in 6 cups of chicken broth.
  9. Add 1 can (14.5 ounces) of diced tomatoes with juices and 2 cups of sliced okra.
  10. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce heat to low and let simmer for minumum 1hr.
  11. Once there is 10-15 minutes left for the Gumbo to be done boil Rice

    12. Serve

  12. Serve over 2 cups of freshly boiled white rice
